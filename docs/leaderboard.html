Product CLI arm · secondary

Same 37 bugs, same MCP bench:grade(), but the agent is the full vendor CLI product (its own planner, tool catalog, and loop) instead of a model wired through our six-tool scaffold. This isolates scaffold effects from model capability — mirroring ExploitBench’s ⟨model, env, CLI⟩ arm.

Scaffold matters — and not always in the product’s favour. Codex (gpt-5.5 + OpenAI’s product scaffold) scored 24/37 under the new raw-output feedback; the same underlying model in the bare-model arm above scored 35/37 even under the older, less-informative blind-bitmap feedback. The product’s extra planning, TodoWrite, and broader tool catalogue did not help on this tightly-defined reproduction task — consistent with ExploitBench’s small/negative scaffold lift. Caveat: in an unrestricted spike Codex web-searched the upstream issue and got 4/4 in 2.5 min; that result was discarded and the locked-down rerun (above) is what we report.

Reading the board

  • gpt-5.5 leads at 35/37, and is the most efficient — ~2 candidate PoCs per solve, vs ~12 for gpt-5.
  • Gemini 3 pro is strong (31 / 30), level with sonnet-4-6; but the older gemini-2.5-pro lands at just 13 — “pro” doesn’t mean newer, and a current flash beats a last-gen pro.
  • opus-4-7 is held back by refusals, not capability: it declined 15/37 episodes of this authorized, already-disclosed-bug reproduction task; same-family sonnet refused 0 and scored 31. We log refusals separately so “won’t” is never scored as “can’t”.
  • Best value: gpt-5.4 — 24/37 at $5.18.
  • Hardest bug: the ICU transliterator memory-leak was solved by no model (its reference PoC reproduces — genuinely hard: a leak detected only at exit, with no crash to guide the agent).

Pilot, single seed. Per-cell results vary run-to-run at temperature 1.0 (a re-run flipped ~13/37 cells), so treat each number as ±several — robust ranking needs multi-seed best-of-N. Full method, per-bug detail, and the harness fixes made during this run are in methodology & data.
